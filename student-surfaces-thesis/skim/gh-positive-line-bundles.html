Positive line bundles and Chern–Weil

⟦NEW⟧ module reservedG–H ch.1 · curve case: board-script §6
Skim layer — recognition, not understanding Orientation only. The curve case of this was touched live in session 1; nothing here has been reconstructed in general.

What this is

Put a Hermitian metric $h$ on a holomorphic line bundle $L$ — a smoothly varying inner product on the fibers. There is then a unique connection compatible with both the metric and the holomorphic structure (the Chern connection — its whole job is to exist and be unique), and its curvature $\Theta_h$ is a $(1,1)$-form on the manifold. Locally, if the metric is $e^{-2\varphi}$ in a trivialization, then $\Theta_h = 2\partial\bar\partial\varphi$ up to constants — curvature is literally the complex Hessian of the weight.

$L$ is positive if some metric makes $\Theta_h$ positive definite everywhere. This is exactly the hypothesis of the Kodaira embedding theorem.

Where it sits

Second step of the analytic route: A1 supplies the universal example ($\mathcal O(1)$ with Fubini–Study). Everything downstream consumes it: A3 assumes positivity, A5 weakens it, and the GAGA bridge must match it with Hartshorne's "ample."

The delta

Chern–Weil: the total curvature is a topological invariant. For any metric $h$ on $L$ over a compact curve, $\frac{i}{2\pi}\int_X \Theta_h = \deg L$ — an integer, independent of $h$. This is the bridge between two books you own: Bott–Tu's characteristic classes ($c_1(L)$ as a cohomology class) and do Carmo's curvature (an honest 2-form you integrate). Positivity is a metric condition, but whether it's achievable is a topological one.

Session 1 used exactly this, aimed at $K_X$: $\deg K_X = 2g-2$ is do Carmo's Gauss–Bonnet "one level up" (board-script §6, with the $\kappa$-vs-$K_X$ notation warning). And Sketch A — redistribute curvature by solving $\Delta\psi = c-\rho_0$ — is the statement "positivity is achievable iff the topological number is positive," on a curve.

Key statement

$c_1(L) = \big[\tfrac{i}{2\pi}\Theta_h\big] \in H^2(M;\mathbb R)$, independent of $h$. $L$ positive $\iff$ $c_1(L)$ contains a positive $(1,1)$-form. Curves: possible $\iff \deg L > 0$. Higher dimension: the content of the Kodaira theorem is that this metric condition already buys a projective embedding.
