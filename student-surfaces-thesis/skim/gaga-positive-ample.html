The bridge: "positive" ↔ "ample"

⟦NEW⟧ module reservedsource TBD — Serre GAGA / Hartshorne App. B
This is the network's honestly-marked soft spot The Kodaira note's algebraic restatement silently uses this correspondence, and its own Checks section flags it as unverified. This page exists so the silent step stays loud.

What this is

The two proof routes don't even state the same theorem until this bridge is built. The analytic hinge says: positive line bundle (a metric with positive curvature, A2) $\Rightarrow$ embedding. Hartshorne's assembly says: ample invertible sheaf (a global-generation property after twisting, II.7 D2) $\Rightarrow$ embedding. "Positive $\iff$ ample" is a real theorem about the comparison of two geometries — the analytic and the algebraic — not a change of vocabulary.

Why it isn't notational

One direction is soft: very ample $\Rightarrow$ the bundle is a pullback of $\mathcal O(1)$, whose Fubini–Study metric is positive (A1) — restrict it. The other direction is the entire Kodaira embedding theorem plus the fact that the resulting analytic embedding lands on an algebraic variety with algebraic structure matching the scheme — which is Chow/GAGA territory. So the bridge consumes the hinge; circularity is avoided only by being careful about which statement is proved in which category first. That bookkeeping is precisely the content of the module.

The curve case shows the pattern in miniature: "$\deg L > 0$" reads as curvature-integral positivity (analytic) or as degree of a divisor class (algebraic), and session 1 §8 ran the two embeddings independently and observed they agree — it never proved the general dictionary. The observation is the phenomenon; the dictionary is the theorem.

Where it sits

Between the two tracks and the hinge node, as the dashed edge in the graph. It is also the natural home of the open question posed in the theorem note: are the two proofs one argument in different clothes, or genuinely different? A precise answer needs this dictionary written down first.

Key statement (target form)

$X$ smooth projective over $\mathbb C$, $L$ a line bundle. Then: $L$ ample (Hartshorne II.7) $\iff$ $L^{an}$ positive (carries a metric with $\Theta_h > 0$). More broadly (GAGA): coherent algebraic sheaves on $X$ and coherent analytic sheaves on $X^{an}$ have the same cohomology — the two categories can't tell each other apart on projective varieties.
