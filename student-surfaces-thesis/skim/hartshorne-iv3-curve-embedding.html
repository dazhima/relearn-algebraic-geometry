Very ampleness on curves: $n \ge 2g+1$ — black box #1

Skim layer — recognition, not understanding This is the one black box currently being opened. Session 1 used the fact live to embed a curve; deriving it is the current unit of work.

What this is

The fact session 1 §8 stood on: for a point $P$ on a compact curve $X$ of genus $g$, the divisor $nP$ with $n \ge 2g+1$ is very ample — its sections embed $X \hookrightarrow \mathbb P^{n-g}$. Combined with Chow's theorem, this proved "compact Riemann surface = algebraic curve." It is also, exactly, the $n=1$ case of the Kodaira embedding theorem: "$\deg > 0$" is the curve reading of "positive line bundle."

Where the difficulty actually lives

The dependency-map.html kill list split this in two. The arithmetic half is already yours: separating points means $h^0(D-P-Q) = h^0(D)-2$, separating tangents means $h^0(D-2P) = h^0(D)-2$, and both follow from Riemann–Roch in the non-special range — session 1 material, done. The conceptual half is the II.7 machine: why "separates points + separates tangent vectors" is equivalent to being a closed immersion (C3/Prop 7.3), and why ample implies a power is very ample (C5/Thm 7.6). That half is what hit trouble on the first pass and is being checked now.

Both derivations of the bound already exist on the board at threshold level: algebraically ($\deg(K - nP) < 0$ kills the correction terms) and analytically (Sketches A+B+C applied to $nP$, giving the identical $n \ge 2g+1$ with zero algebraic input) — board-script §8. What's missing is not the bound; it's owning the embedding criterion underneath it.

Key statement

$X$ smooth projective curve, genus $g$; $D$ a divisor. If $\deg D \ge 2g+1$ then $|D|$ is base-point-free and very ample: $\varphi_{|D|}: X \hookrightarrow \mathbb P^{\deg D - g}$ is a closed immersion. In particular every $P$: $\deg P = 1 > 0 \Rightarrow P$ ample, and $nP$ very ample for $n\ge 2g+1$.

Why this node matters beyond curves

When B2/B3 are built, the general Kodaira statement must recover this exact bound on specialization — that's the consistency check drawn as the red dashed edge in the graph. If the assembly doesn't reproduce $2g+1$, something is wrong upstream. This node is the network's unit test.
