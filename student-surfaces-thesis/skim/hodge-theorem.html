The Hodge theorem — black box #3, and M5b's core

⟦NEW⟧ module reservedG–H ch.0 (also Huybrechts App. + §3.2)
Skim layer — recognition, not understanding Borrowed ahead of schedule in session 1 to derive $g = \frac12 b_1$. This is the analytic track's central deposit — when M5b happens, it happens here.

What this is

Every de Rham cohomology class on a compact Riemannian manifold contains exactly one harmonic representative — one form killed by the Laplacian $\Delta = dd^* + d^*d$. Cohomology, a quotient (forms modulo exact forms), suddenly has canonical elements: you can point at the best representative instead of an equivalence class. Everything the analytic track does downstream — Dolbeault, Serre duality via the star operator, the Hodge decomposition, Kodaira's identities — starts from this one existence-and-uniqueness statement.

You own the two neighbors of this theorem but not the theorem: Bott–Tu gives you de Rham cohomology with no metric at all (purely topological), and do Carmo gives you the metric and a Laplacian, but on functions and for different purposes. The Hodge theorem is the marriage — a metric-dependent normal form for a metric-independent object. Session 1 flagged it as "genuinely new — say so plainly," and that's still exactly right.

Where it sits

Feeds three places in the graph: A3 (vanishing theorems are harmonic-theory arguments), GH-ch3 (harmonic theory for currents), and black box #2 (nondegeneracy of Serre duality is almost automatic once harmonic representatives exist). Session 1 used it once already: harmonic 1-forms = holomorphic ⊕ anti-holomorphic, hence $\dim H^0(\Omega^1) = \tfrac12 b_1 = g$.

The delta

The proof is elliptic PDE theory — the one ingredient in the whole analytic track that is neither complex analysis nor geometry: Sobolev spaces, elliptic regularity, compactness. The honest plan is to treat the PDE input as its own clearly-labeled citation inside the module (the way session 1 cited "the Laplacian surjects onto mean-zero functions" in Sketch A — same elliptic package, simplest instance), and reconstruct everything downstream of it. Decomposing the black box does not mean zero citations; it means knowing exactly what you're standing on.

Key statement

$(M,g)$ compact oriented Riemannian: $\mathcal H^k = \ker\Delta|_{\Omega^k}$ is finite-dimensional and $$H^k_{dR}(M) \cong \mathcal H^k \quad\text{— unique harmonic representative per class,}$$ with the orthogonal decomposition $\Omega^k = \mathcal H^k \oplus d\Omega^{k-1} \oplus d^*\Omega^{k+1}$. On a Riemann surface: $\alpha$ harmonic $\iff$ $\alpha^{1,0}$ holomorphic and $\alpha^{0,1}$ anti-holomorphic.
