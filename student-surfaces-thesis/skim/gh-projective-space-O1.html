Projective space, $\mathcal O(1)$, and the Fubini–Study metric

Skim layer — recognition, not understanding This page tells you what the object is and why it's in the network. Reading it upgrades nothing in the catalog: the module counts as started only when its stub exists, and as understood only after you've computed with it.

What this is

$\mathbb P^n$ is the space of lines through the origin in $\mathbb C^{n+1}$. It carries one distinguished line bundle before anything else happens: the tautological bundle $\mathcal O(-1)$, whose fiber over a point is the line that point represents. Its dual is $\mathcal O(1)$, the hyperplane bundle, and the whole A-route is ultimately about mapping other manifolds into $\mathbb P^n$ so that $\mathcal O(1)$ pulls back to a bundle you care about.

The key concrete fact: global sections of $\mathcal O(1)$ are exactly the linear forms $a_0x_0+\dots+a_nx_n$, and sections of $\mathcal O(d)$ are the degree-$d$ homogeneous polynomials. A section vanishes on a hyperplane (resp. degree-$d$ hypersurface) — that's the divisor–bundle dictionary in its simplest case.

You've met the curve shadow of this already: session 1 computed $h^0(\mathbb P^1,\mathcal O(n))=n+1$ by hand — polynomials of degree $\le n$. Same object, $n$ variables now.

Where it sits

Start of the analytic route: A2 needs it as the prototype of positivity, and Chow's theorem lives on it. It is the analytic twin of Hartshorne's II.7 Thm 7.1 (B1): there, "map to $\mathbb P^n$" is defined by a bundle plus sections; here $\mathbb P^n$ comes first and the bundle is found on it.

The delta — the one genuinely new thing

$\mathcal O(1)$ carries a natural Hermitian metric, inherited from the standard metric on $\mathbb C^{n+1}$, and its curvature form is the Fubini–Study form $\omega_{FS}$ — which is positive everywhere. So the hyperplane bundle isn't just algebraically special; it is the universal example of a positive line bundle, the exact hypothesis of the Kodaira embedding theorem. Curvature (do Carmo's world) attached to a line bundle (Huybrechts's world) — that marriage is new, and it's the whole point.

Key statement

$H^0(\mathbb P^n,\mathcal O(d)) = \{\text{degree-}d\text{ homogeneous polys}\},\ \dim=\binom{n+d}{n}$; and the standard metric on $\mathcal O(1)$ has curvature $\Theta = \omega_{FS} > 0$. On a line $\ell\subset\mathbb P^n$: $\ \tfrac{i}{2\pi}\int_\ell \omega_{FS} = 1 = \deg \mathcal O(1)|_\ell$.
