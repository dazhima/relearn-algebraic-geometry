What this is, and why it's the center of the whole network

The theorem converts a differential-geometric hypothesis into an algebraic-geometric conclusion: curvature positivity in, projective embedding out. It is why "positively curved" and "algebraic" are nearly synonymous words for compact complex manifolds. For this project it is load-bearing three times over: it specializes at $n=1$ to the very fact session 1 used to embed a curve (bb #1); Hartshorne 7.16(c) cites it to keep blow-ups quasi-projective, so the surface chapters stand on it; and it has two genuinely independent proofs — matching the thesis's dual-unlock design exactly.

Key statement

(G–H p.181.) $M$ compact complex manifold, $L \to M$ a positive line bundle. Then there is $k_0$ such that for all $k \ge k_0$, the map $$\iota_{L^k}: M \to \mathbb P^N,\qquad N = \dim H^0(M, L^k) - 1,$$ defined by a basis of sections of $L^k$, is well-defined and an embedding. (Algebraic restatement via the unverified bridge: ample $\Rightarrow$ some power very ample $\Rightarrow$ closed immersion.)

The two routes — every step clickable, every step open

Analytic (Yan leads)

  1. A1 — $\mathbb P^n$, $\mathcal O(1)$, Fubini–Study
  2. A2 — positivity, Chern–Weil
  3. A3 — Kodaira vanishing
  4. A4 — $\bar\partial$ via $L^2$ (partial)
  5. A5 — singular metrics (surfaces)
Strategy (cited): reduce embedding to two surjectivities via exact sequences; both follow from vanishing. Coherent-ideal-sheaf subtlety for $\dim > 1$ — see A3's warning.

Algebraic (student leads)

  1. B1 — sections ⇒ morphism (7.1)
  2. B2 — immersion criterion (7.3)
  3. B3 — ample ⇒ power very ample (7.6) — under attack
  4. B4 — blowing up (D8, 7.13–7.17) (surfaces)
Strategy (cited): no vanishing theorem at all — "ample" is defined by generation, and 7.6 is a direct affine-patching construction.
The open question, kept open on purpose: are these the same proof wearing different clothes, or structurally different arguments reaching the same conclusion? The theorem note explicitly defers this until both routes exist. Resist answering early — that comparison is thesis material.
