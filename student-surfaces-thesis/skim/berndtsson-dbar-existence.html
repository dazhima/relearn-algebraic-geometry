$\bar\partial$-existence via $L^2$ estimates

partial — Sketches A–C done live🎤 conference coreBerndtsson, materials/7nynot.pdf · black box #4
Skim layer — recognition, not understanding The one-variable case was built on the board in session 1 (§6). Beyond one variable everything is still cited. This is the single most load-bearing phrase you will hear at the SCV conference.

What this is

The equation $\bar\partial u = f$ is the analyst's version of "correct a smooth object into a holomorphic one": if you can always solve it (with control on the size of $u$), you can manufacture holomorphic sections at will — take a rough candidate with the behavior you want, compute its $\bar\partial$-error, solve, subtract. Session 1's Sketch C did exactly this to build a meromorphic function with a prescribed pole.

Hörmander's theorem says: positivity of the weight guarantees solvability. In one variable — the version already on your board — if $\Delta\varphi > 0$ everywhere, then $\bar\partial u = f$ has a solution with $\int|u|^2e^{-\varphi} \le \int \frac{|f|^2}{\Delta\varphi}e^{-\varphi}$.

The two-step proof you saw (Sketch B): one integration by parts gives the a priori inequality; Hahn–Banach + Riesz turns the inequality into a solution. No PDE machinery beyond that — the analysis is soft once the identity is right. That structure survives in all dimensions.

Where it sits

The engine room of the analytic route: A3's vanishing is "$\bar\partial$ is solvable in the relevant degree"; A5 runs the same estimates with singular weights; the horizon's Ohsawa–Takegoshi extension theorem and everything Guan–Zhou is this tool, sharpened. Hörmander's estimate is black box #4 in the log — the only one marked partial.

The delta — what's new beyond one variable

In several variables the Laplacian of the weight is replaced by the full complex Hessian $\big(\frac{\partial^2\varphi}{\partial z_j\partial\bar z_k}\big)$ — positivity now means plurisubharmonic, strictly — and on a manifold the integration-by-parts identity becomes the Bochner–Kodaira–Nakano identity, where the curvature of the bundle metric (A2) enters as the good term. Same skeleton as Sketch B, heavier algebra in step one. Berndtsson's notes are organized exactly along this ramp.

Key statement (shape to recognize)

$(M,\omega)$ Kähler, $L$ with metric of curvature $\Theta \ge \varepsilon\omega > 0$: for every $\bar\partial$-closed $(n,1)$-form $f$ with values in $L$, there is $u$ with $\bar\partial u = f$ and $\|u\|^2_{L^2} \le \tfrac{1}{\varepsilon}\|f\|^2_{L^2}$. Positivity in, solvability out, with a constant you can track — the "with a constant" part is what the conference cares about.
