Singular hermitian metrics (Demailly)

⟦NEW⟧ module reservedmaterials/bayreuth.pdf — on disk · surfaces only
Skim layer — recognition, not understanding The surface-case tool. Not needed for curves; becomes load-bearing at blow-ups. Statement level only.

What this is

A singular hermitian metric on $L$ is $h = h_0e^{-2\varphi}$ where $h_0$ is a smooth metric and the weight $\varphi$ is merely locally integrable — allowed to be $-\infty$ somewhere, typically like $\log|s|$ along a divisor. The curvature is then not a smooth form but a closed positive current: a differential form with distribution coefficients (the object G–H ch.3 introduces; think of "integration along a divisor" as a legitimate $(1,1)$-form, the way Bott–Tu lets a submanifold be a de Rham class).

Where it sits

Last analytic step before the hinge, needed only for the surface half (step A5). Downstream it is the doorway to the entire horizon layer: multiplier ideals $\mathscr J(\varphi)$ are defined from these weights, Nadel vanishing is Kodaira vanishing (A3) for these metrics, and everything in Boucksom / Demailly–Kollár speaks this language.

The delta — why smooth metrics stop being enough

On a blow-up, the exceptional divisor $E$ has $E^2 = -1$: no smooth metric on the relevant bundle can be strictly positive everywhere — negativity is topologically locked in along $E$ (Chern–Weil, A2, forbids it). Demailly's move: stop asking for smoothness. Concentrate the negativity into a singularity of the weight, keep the current positive, and run the $L^2$ machinery (A4) anyway — the estimates survive singular weights, that's the miracle. The price: solutions acquire mandatory vanishing along the singularity, and bookkeeping that price is exactly what multiplier ideals will do.

Compare Sketch A on a curve: there, curvature could be freely redistributed because the total was positive. On a surface the total can be "positive" while a curve inside carries irreducibly negative self-intersection — redistribution hits a wall, singular weights go through it.

Key statement (shape to recognize)

$L$ pseudoeffective / big $\iff$ $L$ carries a singular metric with curvature current $\ge 0$ (resp. $\ge \varepsilon\omega$). Demailly's program: replace "positive line bundle" everywhere by "singular metric with positive curvature current + control of the singularity," and the embedding/vanishing theory extends to bundles that are only generically positive.

To go further

Source: materials/bayreuth.pdf (Demailly, Singular hermitian metrics on positive line bundles) — already on disk; it also contains multiplier ideals and Nadel vanishing, i.e. the first two horizon modules live in the same paper.

What upgrades this to a module: stub + the canonical E-entry: the weight $\varphi = \log|z|$ on the disk — compute its curvature current (Poincaré–Lelong: $dd^c\log|z| = $ point mass), then the surface version along $E$.
