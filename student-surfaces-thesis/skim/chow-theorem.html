Chow's theorem — black box #5

⟦NEW⟧ module reservedG–H ch.1 · used in board-script §8
Skim layer — recognition, not understanding The last step of session 1's embedding argument — cited in one breath, never derived.

What this is

Anything compact and analytic sitting inside projective space is automatically algebraic: a closed analytic subvariety of $\mathbb P^N$ (locally cut out by convergent power series) is in fact cut out by polynomials. In session 1 §8 this was the closer: the linear system $|nP|$ gave a holomorphic embedding $X \hookrightarrow \mathbb P^{n-g}$, and Chow upgraded the image from "complex-analytic curve in $\mathbb P^{n-g}$" to "projective algebraic curve" — finishing the proof that a compact Riemann surface is an algebraic curve.

Why it should feel surprising

Transcendental data has infinitely many degrees of freedom; polynomials have finitely many. The theorem says projective space is so rigid that the gap closes by itself. Contrast the non-compact picture you know well: in $\mathbb C^2$, the graph of $w = e^z$ is a perfectly good closed analytic subvariety and is nowhere near algebraic. Compactness plus the positivity of $\mathcal O(1)$ (the A1 geometry) is what forbids such things in $\mathbb P^N$.

Same phenomenon, one dimension down, already on your board: a holomorphic function on a compact curve is constant (max modulus — used in §7's base case). Chow is the statement that on compact projective objects, "analytic" keeps collapsing to "algebraic" in every codimension.

Where it sits

Needed by the curve-floor argument (bb #1's §8 use) and by the GAGA bridge — Chow is the prototype and easiest instance of the GAGA principle, and any honest treatment of "positive ↔ ample" passes nearby. G–H prove it via the proper mapping theorem / the structure of analytic sets; Serre's GAGA reproves and vastly generalizes it sheaf-theoretically.

Key statement

Chow. Every closed analytic subvariety $V \subset \mathbb P^N(\mathbb C)$ is algebraic: $V = Z(F_1,\dots,F_m)$ for homogeneous polynomials $F_i$. Consequently a holomorphic map between projective varieties is a morphism, and holomorphic embeddings into $\mathbb P^N$ have algebraic images.
