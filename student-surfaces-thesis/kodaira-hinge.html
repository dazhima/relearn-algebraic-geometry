The hinge: Kodaira embedding, two proofs, one destination

The main stem of this page is a single theorem and its two proofs. Everything before it (session 1's curves) is a special case of it; everything after it (surfaces — Hartshorne Ch. V, Griffiths–Harris Ch. III–IV) needs it. The point of walking both proof routes — one algebraic, one analytic — is not the theorem itself: it's that the two routes, between them, force exactly the fundamental toolkit that studying surfaces requires. The proofs are the curriculum.

The prediction, fulfilled dependency-map.html closed with this heads-up for Stem 6:
"Expect one genuinely new nonskippable ingredient at Stem 6 — a vanishing theorem (Serre vanishing, or Kodaira vanishing) — to close the surface embedding argument."
That ingredient now has a name and an address: the Kodaira Embedding Theorem (Griffiths–Harris p. 181), whose analytic proof runs exactly through a vanishing theorem. And it isn't only the surface story — the curve fact session 1 already used live ($\deg P = 1 \Rightarrow P$ ample; $n\ge 2g+1 \Rightarrow nP$ very ample) is its $n=1$ shadow. The kill list grew up into a theorem.
Session 1 — happened, and went further than planned (2026-07-02) The pre-session plan (thesis-guide.html, now stale — it still says "tomorrow") called for stating two theorems and logging black boxes. What actually happened (board-script.md): Serre duality stated with its residue pairing (§3); the $\deg D > 2g-2$ vanishing proved twice — algebraically via duality (§5) and analytically via Gauss–Bonnet + Hörmander (§6, Sketches A–C); Riemann–Roch derived twice, by the subscheme sequence (§4) and by point-induction (§7); and, closing the loop, a dual proof of "algebraic curve = compact Riemann surface" (§8). Five results were cited live without derivation — they are the black-box log below, all still open.

The ordering is deliberate, not incidental For any given black box, the student is sent into the harder, more scheme-theoretic source (Hartshorne) first — even where Griffiths–Harris would be more accessible given his background (do Carmo, Bott–Tu, Huybrechts ch.1 are all closer to G–H's register). The point: build the rigorous version before a geometric picture exists to lean on. The analytic/G–H route arrives second, as an independent confirmation of the same fact from the opposite direction — the same dual-unlock pattern as M5a/M5b — not as the easier way in.

Blow-up caution — a documented trouble spot, do not re-trip on it The blow-up is $\operatorname{Proj}$ of the Rees algebra $\bigoplus_{d\ge0}\mathscr I^d$ (II.7 #D8). The existing deformation-to-normal-cone brick is about the normal cone — $\operatorname{Spec}$ of the associated graded $\bigoplus \mathscr I^d/\mathscr I^{d+1}$. Related constructions, genuinely different objects: one is a birational modification of $X$, the other is the degeneration target sitting over the center. dependency-map.html's Stem-5 card points at the brick as background; the brick is not the blow-up construction itself.

The horizon past the hinge — why the analytic track is oversized on purpose

The A-track builds more analytic machinery than the surface case strictly needs. That's deliberate: the same tools are the working language of the several-complex-variables world this project points at next. Two concrete targets, both downstream of A4 + A5, both not started — listed here so the stem is read as an on-ramp, not a closed loop.

Byproduct target · the student · upcoming SCV conference

Follow the talks: $L^2$ estimates & multiplier ideal sheaves

  • $L^2$ estimates — already seeded: board-script §6 Sketch B is Hörmander in one variable; A4 (Berndtsson) is its general form. This is the single most load-bearing phrase at an SCV conference.
  • Multiplier ideal sheaves $\mathscr J(\varphi)$ — germs $f$ with $|f|^2e^{-2\varphi}$ locally integrable: the object that makes A5's singular metrics usable. One small step past A5, and the standing vocabulary of the field.
  • Nadel vanishing — Kodaira vanishing (A3) upgraded to singular metrics, with $\mathscr J(\varphi)$ absorbing the singularities. Recognizing it as "A3, singular version" is exactly conference-following ability.
Recognition mode, not mastery — the goal is that talk titles and blackboard incantations resolve to known objects. Per-talk prep gets its own talk-init cards when the program is out.
